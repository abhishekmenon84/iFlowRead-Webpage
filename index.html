01 — Write

Every script,
ready when you are.

Drop in a script from Notes, a voice memo, or your own typing. iFlowRead keeps every draft organized by read time and word count — recent, favorited, or archived — so you're never digging for the right file before a shoot.

02 — Record

Full screen.
Full focus.

Your lines fill the frame edge to edge, scrolling at the pace you set — 0.25× for a slow, deliberate read, up to 3× when you're warmed up. A small picture-in-picture window keeps your face in view, so the only thing your audience sees is you, present and on point.

Start a take straight from your script — no extra screens, no lost place.

03 — Review

Know which
take to keep.

Every take is scored automatically as you record. When take 14 lands at 100% and take 9 sits at 68%, you already know which one goes to the edit — no scrubbing through fourteen clips to find out.

Star your favorite, save it straight to Photos, and move on to the next line.
